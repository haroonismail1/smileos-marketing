SmileOS vs UK dental software

An honest look at how SmileOS compares.

UK dental practice software has serious incumbents — Dentally, Software of Excellence, Carestream — and a sharp AI specialist (Kiroku). Here's where SmileOS fits, where it doesn't, and why founder-led, consultation-first software exists in the first place.

Positioning

SmileOS isn't trying to be another PMS.

Dentally, EXACT and Carestream are practice management systems with decades of breadth. SmileOS is a consultation-first, AI-assisted platform for cosmetic and orthodontic-focused private practices. Different shape, different goal.

If you need full NHS PMS

Stay with Dentally, EXACT, or Carestream. They've spent decades on FP17s, NHS contracts, charting, recall. We don't try to be them.

If you just need AI notes

Kiroku is excellent at that — and we won't pretend to win the notes-only battle. Use them if AI dictation is the whole problem you're solving.

If you run cosmetic/ortho lists

SmileOS is built for the full consultation-to-treatment flow: notes, consent, estimate, payment, treatment plan. Founder-led by a UK clinical director.

Right fit?

SmileOS is right for you if…

You run cosmetic, ortho, or mixed private lists

The consultation is the most important conversation in your week. SmileOS treats it that way.

You're sick of consent & estimate friction

You want consent and pricing inside the consult, not bolted on after. SmileOS is built around that.

You want a direct line to the founder

Not a ticket queue, not "raise it with support". WhatsApp the dentist building it.

You want founder pricing locked in for life

First 50 UK practices get founder rates that don't move with public launch. Worth more than a discount.

SmileOS is not right for you if…

NHS-heavy practice with FP17 dependency

We don't do NHS claim submission. Dentally / EXACT / Carestream are the right tools for that workflow.

You need legacy integrations on day one

We integrate where it matters (Stripe, email, calendar). If you need 20-year-old radiology or charting tools wired in today, we're not there yet.
